First Research Task


With it now in mind that it is people that will be our primary object of study, let us now start with the first Studies of Aotearoa/New Zealand subject of research; YOU!

Finding out about your background and where you come from can help with understanding where you fit into the world. It can also provide context to how we interpret history from a personal perspective.

There are many ways to find out about your whakapapa or genealogy. These may include talking to family members or close friends in the community (particularly elders). Maybe within your family there are documents or records relating to past family members. There may be photos kept. See how much new information you can find and how far back you can trace your ancestry.

TIP: Many libraries and museums in New Zealand host research centres. These can be interesting depositories for local history and heritage resources. Many of these resources can also be accessed online. Some ancestry website databases are also accessible through local libraries. See the Puke Ariki website for a Taranaki based example.
