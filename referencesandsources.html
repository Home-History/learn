3. References and Sources


Now we will look at the “tools” of the historian; sources. This is how we claim to know what we know about the past. Without sources, we only know what we do not know.

Sources come in many forms; that is, a source is anything (or anyone) that informs the historian to the question being asked. How many different types of sources can you think of? Take some time to come up with a list and post it in the text box below.







Are all sources created equal?

All sources will have their own advantages and disadvantages. In general, no one type of source is essentially better than other types; it will depend on the context of how the source is being used and in relation to what topic. Later we will look at a common disadvantage all sources potentially share.

For now, we will just identify two large categories sources can fall into.
