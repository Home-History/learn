4. Migrations and Settlement





Throughout human history there is a pattern of migration, from overland out of Africa, to further continents and islands in voyages across the seas and oceans. Today, almost every conceivable land capable of being colonised for human habitation has been. In this respect Aotearoa is somewhat special, in that it is widely regarded to be the last land known to be settled by people. Now we will take a brief look at some theories of how the tangata whenua may have come to occupy this country we call New Zealand.
