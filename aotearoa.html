"Aotearoa"



Aotearoa is the Māori name that we today use alongside New Zealand to refer to the archipelago of islands that make up the country we live in. Unlike "New Zealand", there is no clear single account of its original usage in reference to the country by name. Therefore, to consider the origins of the name Aotearoa, historians have to consult a wider variety sources to find more definitive answers.





























Nu Tireni



The first known Māori name used to refer to the wider landmass in a written record is Nu Tireni, a transliteration of New Zealand (itself, as we know, an anglicised version from the original Dutch, by the 19th c. already in common usage). This appeared prominently in the Declaration of Independence (He Whakaputanga) in 1835, and in 1840 on the Treaty of Waitangi as Nu Tirani, among other early documents.
