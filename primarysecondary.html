Primary Sources



Sources that are contemporary to the period being studied are considered to be primary. They are usually first hand or eye witness accounts. Common examples of historical primary sources are official documents, letters, diaries and newspapers. Speeches, video and photos from the studied period can also be primary sources. Art can also contribute to historical understanding; commissioned portraits are commonly used primary sources. Even buildings and other significant physical structures can contribute to our understanding of architecture and cultural heritage.

Archives New Zealand, located in Wellington, is our largest national depository of primary sources. Other large collections of primary sources can be found at the National Library of New Zealand, as well as numerous museums, libraries and collections throughout the country.

















Secondary Sources



Secondary sources are works that draw on primary sources (or other secondary sources) to describe, explain, or argue a historical point of view or account of past events.

Like primary sources, secondary sources can come in a wide variety of media. Some common forms of secondary sources include books, journals or documentaries, among others. This Home History resource itself can be considered a secondary source.
