Staten Landt cont.


After coming upon the Australian Island of Tasmania (which, at the time, he named Van Diemen’s Land), Tasman continued eastward until on December 13th, 1642, they sighted a “great, high bold land”. Observing no sign of habitation, they continued north and east along the coast, seeking a convenient harbour to anchor. When within a mile from the coast, seeing smoke rising from inland, they found a large, open bay. On the morning of the 18th, there they weighed anchor.2 It is here that the first documented account of a Maori and European encounter occurred, as described in Tasman’s journals...
