References


References are a way to show the audience of a work where the source information has come from. There are different styles of referencing, but usually a reference will appear as a footnote at the bottom of a page or as an endnote at the end of a piece of work.*

Referencing should be used when a statement has been made that requires verifying, or someone else’s work has been quoted or reproduced in part or in full. If you look back on previous pages in this resource you will find some have paragraphs have footnotes with the source referenced. Some of the images used are also referenced with captions on mouseover.

The following content will introduce a familiar style of referencing.


* There are certain formats to follow when using an established academic referencing style, but this is unimportant to the kaupapa of this particular course.

** In your research you may come across the reference term ibid. Ibid. is an abbreviation of the Latin “ibidem,” meaning “in the same place.” It is used in citations to quickly cite a source that has already been cited in full in a previous footnote or endnote.
