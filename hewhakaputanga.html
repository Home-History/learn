New Zealand’s First Flag


As was mentioned in the video on the previous page, the adoption of a flag in New Zealand was borne from the necessity for merchant ships to comply with British maritime law. Adopted in 1834, what would become known as the flag of the United Tribes of New Zealand was chosen by northern rangatira, a flag originally designed by Henry Williams to represent the New Zealand Church Missionary Society. The flag prominently features the St. George cross, the national flag of England.




Today, the flag of the United Tribes is sometimes flown as an alternative to the present day Blue Ensign, particuarly by those dissaffected with post-Treaty of Waitangi national political structures. It is the flag used to reperesent the Wakaminenga Māori Government, the general assembly of the United Tribes operating according to their national constitution “He Wakaputanga o Tino Rangatiratanga o Nu Tireni 1835" (Declaration of Independence o Nu Tireni 1835).
