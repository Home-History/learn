Research Task


By now you will be getting the idea that names given to countries, cities or landmarks can vary dramatically. Some, particularly colonised lands, will bear the name from the place of origin of the colonists (eg. New Plymouth, Stratford, Inglewood). Others will be named in honour of notable people of the colonial homeland (eg. Wellington, Queenstown). Many, especially Māori place names, will describe geographical features.

There are many other factors that go into the naming of places. Take some time to think of a place, and research where its name comes from. It can be anywhere in New Zealand. It may just be the street you live on.

Think of some of the sources you used to research your own family. Some of these may be useful again for this task.

TIP: If researching Māori places names, cultural atlases can provide much historical information on places. Kā Huru Manu covers over 1000 Maori locations of interest to Ngai Tahu throughout Te Waipounamu/ Te Waka o Aoraki/ Te Waka o Maui/ South Island.
