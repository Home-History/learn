Parihaka


Parihaka is a settlement that sits west at the foot of Mt. Taranaki. While existing today as an independent community dedicated to the values and principles of its founders, in the public conscience of many New Zealanders, it is commonly remembered as a pacifist settlement and site of invasion by the colonial government in the late 19th century.

Next we will look at how the events that occurred were interpreted by different sources at various moments in time, and how these competed to form a narrative around Parihaka.
